Cotton In Our Clothing

The heavy use of chemicals in cotton farming is causing diseases and premature death among cotton farmers, along with massive freshwater and ocean water pollution and soil degradation. The largely untested impacts of these chemicals on both the land and human health are beginning to be questioned by those working in the industry. As our skin is the largest organ, these chemicals are passed into the bloodstream of the people wearing these clothes.



Cotton represents nearly 50% of the total fiber used to make clothing today.

More than 90% of that cotton is now genetically modified, using vast amounts of water as well as chemicals.

Cotton production is now responsible for 27% of insecticide use worldwide.
